# Polymarket 129-Market Scan Analysis

**Date**: 2026-02-17
**Pipeline**: 81 markets cached (windowed API) → 129 single-asset backtests → 100 pairs → walk-forward on top 20
**Slippage**: 1% per trade | **Timeframe**: 1h | **Data**: Real hourly (windowed CLOB API)

---

## Executive Summary

**pm-mean-reversion is the only viable strategy**, hitting 50% profitability across 129 markets. However, walk-forward validation reveals **80% of top results are overfit** — only 4/20 survive out-of-sample testing. The other two strategies (information-edge, correlation-pairs) should be abandoned for Polymarket.

The key finding: **prediction market mean reversion works on markets that oscillate without converging**. Markets that resolve to 0 or 1 during the backtest period break the strategy, explaining both the high in-sample hit rate and the brutal walk-forward failure rate.

**Common trait of survivors**: Price continued to oscillate in the out-of-sample period without converging to 0 or 1.

### The 16 Failures — Two Failure Modes

**Mode 1: Price Convergence (0 OOS trades)** — 6/16 failures
- Vanta IPO, ETH $5500, ETH $7000, Jamie Davis, etc.
- Price hit 0.95+ or <0.05 in the last 30%, stopped oscillating
- BB width collapsed below minBBWidth threshold → no entries
- **Root cause**: These markets approached resolution, killing mean reversion

**Mode 2: Regime Change (negative OOS Sharpe)** — 10/16 failures
- BNB $500 (IS 6.74 → OOS -3.18), Wizards (IS 4.47 → OOS -1.33)
- Market changed behavior: volatility shifted, new trend started
- Parameters optimized for IS period didn't fit OOS dynamics
- **Root cause**: Classic overfitting to a specific volatility regime

### Zcash $600: Legitimate or Fluke?
- IS Sharpe: **-2.65** (negative! lost money in training period)
- OOS Sharpe: **8.81** (massive gain in test period)
- This is a **regime change in the strategy's favor** — the first 70% of data had a trend the strategy bet against, then the market oscillated in the last 30%
- **Verdict**: Likely legitimate but unreliable. We can't predict when a market will shift to mean-reverting behavior. The IS loss means the default params would have discouraged us from trading it, so this "survivor" isn't actually tradeable in practice.

## 3. What Makes Mean Reversion Work on PM?

### Market Characteristics That Predict Success

**Good markets (oscillators):**
- Binary events with **uncertain resolution timing** (CBOE regulatory, Fields Medal, political events with unclear timelines)
- Price stays in **0.20–0.80 range** throughout most of the period
- Sufficient **liquidity for regular price movement** (not dead flat)
- Long time until resolution (months, not weeks)

**Bad markets (convergers):**
- Events with **clear resolution trajectory** (crypto price targets — either obviously going to hit or not)
- Price trends monotonically toward 0 or 1
- Markets near resolution date
- Low volume / infrequent trades

### The Fundamental PM Mean Reversion Edge

The edge comes from **overreaction to noise in uncertain markets**. When a market is genuinely uncertain (40-60% probability), each new piece of information pushes the price, but the market tends to overcorrect. Bollinger Bands capture these overcorrections.

This edge **disappears** when:
1. The market's true probability shifts decisively (news event resolves uncertainty)
2. The market converges toward resolution
3. Price enters extreme zones where the avoidExtremes filter blocks trading

## 4. Market Selection Filter (Proposed)

Based on the scan results, a pre-trade market filter should check:

1. **Price Range**: Current price between 0.15–0.85 (avoid markets already converging)
2. **Volatility**: BB width > 0.08 over last 20 bars (enough oscillation to trade)
3. **Time to Resolution**: > 30 days remaining (avoid convergence zone)
4. **Recent Activity**: Volume > 0 in at least 50% of last 20 hourly bars
5. **Historical Oscillation**: Price has crossed the SMA at least 3 times in last 100 bars
6. **Avoid Categories**: Skip crypto price targets (too trendy), sports results (too short-lived)

### Estimated Impact
If we had applied filters 1-3 to the 129 scanned markets:
- Would have eliminated most of the "0 OOS trades" failures
- Would have filtered out converging crypto markets (ETH $5500, ETH $7000, BNB)
- Likely would increase WF pass rate from 20% to ~40-50%

## 5. Slippage Assessment (1%)

### Is 1% Realistic?

Polymarket uses an order book (CLOB), not AMM. Slippage depends on:
- **Order size**: $100-500 typically 0.5-1% slippage; $5000+ could be 2-5%
- **Market liquidity**: Top markets (political) have deep books; niche markets are thin
- **Execution method**: Limit orders = 0% slippage but may not fill; market orders = variable

**For the top mean-reversion markets** (which tend to be higher-volume):
- 1% is **reasonable for moderate position sizes** ($100-1000)
- For larger positions ($5000+), need to consider market impact
- **Recommendation**: Use 1% as base, but add a dynamic slippage model based on market volume

### Impact Analysis
At 1% slippage per trade with avg 2 trades per round trip:
- Round-trip cost: ~2% of position value
- Top markets (Sharpe > 3): Edge is large enough to absorb this
- Marginal markets (Sharpe 1-2): Slippage eats significant portion of edge
- **Only trade markets where expected profit > 4%** (the minProfitPct filter is crucial)

## 6. Actionable Next Steps (Ranked by Impact)

### Priority 1: Build a Dynamic Market Selector
**Impact: HIGH** | **Effort: MEDIUM**

Implement a real-time market screening tool that:
- Fetches active PM markets daily
- Applies the selection filters from Section 4
- Ranks markets by "oscillation score" (price crossover frequency × BB width × volume)
- Alerts when new markets become mean-reversion-friendly

This is the single highest-impact improvement. Market selection matters more than strategy parameters.

### Priority 2: Parameter Optimization Per Market Category
**Impact: MEDIUM** | **Effort: LOW**

The current strategy uses one set of defaults for all markets. Different market types need different params:
- **Political/regulatory** (CBOE, Fields Medal): Longer BB period (30-40), wider bands (2.5σ)
- **Crypto price targets**: Don't trade (too trendy) or use very tight bands (1.5σ) with quick exits
- **Pop culture/entertainment**: Standard params (20 period, 2.0σ)

Run grid search on the 4 WF survivors + the 41 high-confidence markets grouped by category.

### Priority 3: Live Paper Trading on Top Markets
**Impact: HIGH** | **Effort: MEDIUM**

Set up paper trading on the 4 walk-forward survivors + a rotating pool of markets that pass the selection filter. This is the only way to validate real-world performance before risking capital.

Key metrics to track:
- Fill rate (what % of signals get executed)
- Actual slippage vs assumed 1%
- Time-in-market vs time-out
- Rolling Sharpe over 7-day windows

### Priority 4: Abandon Information-Edge and Correlation-Pairs
**Impact: LOW (saves wasted effort)** | **Effort: NONE**

Stop investing development time in these strategies for PM. They are fundamentally mismatched:
- Information-edge assumes momentum patterns; PM prices are event-driven
- Correlation-pairs assumes economic linkage; PM markets are independent

### Priority 5: Explore Alternative Strategy: Trend Following Near Resolution
**Impact: SPECULATIVE** | **Effort: HIGH**

Mean reversion fails near resolution, but **trend following** might work:
- When a market crosses 0.80 or drops below 0.20, it often continues toward 0/1
- A simple "ride the convergence" strategy could complement mean reversion
- Entry: Price crosses above 0.80 (or below 0.20) with increasing volume
- Exit: At 0.95 (or 0.05) or at resolution
- This would capture the exact regime where mean reversion fails
